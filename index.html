I am an astronomy PhD student and fellow of the International Max Planck Research School on Astronomy and Cosmic Physics at the University of Heidelberg (IMPRS). I work in the Galactic Nuclei Group (Nadine Neumayer) at the Max Planck Institute for Astronomy (MPIA) in Heidelberg.

Previously, I obtained a Bachelors and Masters degree in physics at the University of Heidelberg. During my undergraduate studies I spent time at the European Southern Observatory in Chile and the Space Telescope Science Institue in Baltimore, USA.

My research focuses on observational studies of globular clusters but also the development of astronomical instrumentation. I am currently involved in the development of the SDSS-V Local Volume Mapper, a novel wide-field integral field spectroscopy survey instrument. For details you may take a look at the RESEARCH section.
